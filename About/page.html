â€‹Jerald Washington began his audio journey in the middle of his freshman year of high school in 2005. Jerald's time at Brown Middle School in the years before truly shaped his love for the recording studio arts. Being involved in the Dallas Austin Foundation's efforts to expose the local youth to the benefits of learning the recording arts opened Jerald's eyes to the possibilties.

It was in this year that Jerald downloaded FL Studio 6, Demo of Reason 3, and began to immerse himself in the art of production outside of the after school programs. The often ill-fated attempts to make music without any rounded out skills and knowledge would test Jerald's resolve. Jerald spent the next couple years networking, learning more and more of the recording, mixing and mastering process throughout his high school years. In 2008, during his senior year of high school, Jerald would began interning at Hush Management LLC, where he would learn about the music business when not in school. The work ethic learned here would open up Jerald's world to professional recording studios within the city. 

Jerald enrolled at Clark Atlanta University in Fall of 2009 after completing his 1st internship. It was here that he would build habits resembling that of an audio engineer, despite being a business administration major. Jerald found himself recording students and non-students alike while staying in the dorms. This time would solidify Jerald's love for the craft and business of audio engineering. Jerald would leave CAU in Spring of 2010, landing at Full Sail University Online in Fall of 2010. This decision was for the best since his time at Clark gave him an opportunity to soul search and truly focus on the difficult road to becoming a solid audio engineer.

Matriculating the grueling but rewarding accelerated pace at Full Sail was no easy task for Jerald. The balance of undergrad and work life balance for a self-employed audio engineer can be murky waters to say the least. Half a year before graduating from Full Sail University, Jerald would begin work as an intern for Esquire One Entertainment Group working out of their Pre-Production Room located within ICON Studios in the heart of Atlanta. Jerald made this once in a lifetime opportunity count and eventually worked his way up to Lead Audio Engineer for Esquire One. Jerald began to collaborate with several buzzing indie artists, acts such as Skooda Chose, Jawz of Life (Dugeon Family 2nd Gen), Capri, Honey The Hippie, BenniB to name a few.

Jerald would take the opportunity to further his education years after completing his Bachelor's of Science in Music Production at Full Sail University, by enrolling at The University of Miami in Spring of 2015. Jerald once again balanced work and life while completing the necessary credits to earn his Master of Music in Music Business and Entertainment Industries in May of 2016. He would meet a fellow music business minded fellow alum and they would mesh together right away to collaborate on some projects. Jerald and his UM classmate would collaborate on mixing/mastering projects for Carbon Fiber Music from 2018 to the present day. These passionate filled projects based in the Urbano Latin genre would challenge Jerald to master audio for a lane he had little expereince in. Working on radio edits for songs en espanol, quick turnaround mixes and masters, hightened Jerald's love for the craft of audio even more. "These challenges and the ability to step up and face them make us who we are." - JW

Since graduating from UM, Jerald has made it his life's work to expand the reach of quality audio for everyone. One size does NOT fit all when it comes to an audio. It requires someone who has been tested throughout the recording/mixing and mastering process. Jerald continues to work as a freelance Audio Engineer, prioritzing making quality audio accessible for anyone who wants access.
